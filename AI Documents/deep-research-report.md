## The environment I would build

I would build this as a **remote-first dev box**. Your laptop stays a thin client for editing, Git, and terminal access. The scraper itself runs on the VPS, which means the traffic generated by the scraper comes from the VPS and not from your home internet connection. That is exactly what Remote SSH and remote Dev Container workflows are good at: your editor attaches to the remote machine, and the toolchain lives where the code executes. Do note the important boundary here: this protects the IP of the **code you run remotely**; it does **not** automatically proxy your ordinary local web browsing if you open target sites in your desktop browser. ## Safety guardrails that actually reduce accidental blocking

You specifically said you want to avoid accidental trouble, so your environment should enforce **compliance-oriented defaults**. Start with robots awareness, but do not confuse that with permission. RFC 9309 standardizes the Robots Exclusion Protocol and is explicit that these rules are **not a form of access authorization**. In Python, `urllib.robotparser` is built to answer whether a given user agent may fetch a URL according to the siteŌĆÖs published `robots.txt`. In Scrapy, the robots middleware plus `ROBOTSTXT_OBEY` provides the same kind of baseline protection, and `ROBOTSTXT_USER_AGENT` lets you match the robot user-agent check to the user-agent you actually send. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn9search1Ņłéturn5search3Ņłéturn18search10Ņłü

The second guardrail is **explicitly conservative concurrency**. ScrapyŌĆÖs AutoThrottle is designed to adjust crawl speed based on load, which is exactly what you want early on. Direct HTTP clients are where teams get into trouble by accident: aiohttp documents a default connector limit of **100 total connections** and **no per-host limit by default**, and HTTPX documents a default maximum of **100 total connections** as well. For development against third-party sites, that is far looser than I would be comfortable with. Set low per-host and total limits yourself, and only raise them after you have permission and a clear reason to do so. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn8search0Ņłéturn8search2Ņłéturn8search14Ņłéturn20search0Ņłü
